Introduction

The Ontologizer is a stand-alone Java application for exploration, visualization, and statistical analysis of biological data using Gene Ontology (GO). Users can analyze large data sets such as those that typically arise from microarray experiments (among many other scenarios) for annotations to GO terms in order to gain an overview of the biological characteristics of the data they are analyzing.

This help section intends to give a brief overview of the most important topics involved in GO analysis. More detailed information is available at the homepage of the Gene Ontology consortium or the homepage of the Ontologizer project.

Gene Ontology

The Gene Ontology (GO) Consortium (http://www.geneontology.org) provides a structured, controlled vocabulary for describing the roles of genes and gene products in any organism. Three separate ontologies are provided: Biological process describes the biological objective to which the gene product contributes, Molecular function describes the biochemical activity of a gene product and Cellular component refers to the place in the cell in which a gene product exerts its activity (Ashburner et al., 2000). The terms in the GO database form a directed acyclic graph, in which terms are children of one or several more general terms. For instance, the term "DNA replication" (GO:0006260) is a child of the term "DNAreplication and cell cycle" (GO:0000067) and also of the term "S phase of mitotic cell cycle" (GO:0000084).

Annotations

The GO Terms do not themselves describe specific genes or gene products. Rather, collaborating databases generate gene association files consisting of links between genes or gene products and GO terms. Genes and gene products are annotated at the most specific level possible, but are considered to share the attributes of all the parent nodes (true-path rule). At present, association files have been made available for over 30 organisms, including human, mouse, yeast and Caenorhabditis elegans.

Population Files and Statistical Testing

For many types of analysis, users may want merely a listing of annotation counts in their dataset(s). It can also be useful to know if the number of genes annotated to any given term is more than expected by chance, i.e., if there is a statistically significant overrepresentation of annotations to GO terms in the dataset.

To perform this type of analysis, one needs to have a population dataset against which the annotations in the study dataset can be compared. For instance, if one is looking at a set of genes that are overexpressed in a microarray experiment, the population set might be all genes on the chip. If one is looking at all human proteins that contain a given sequence motif, the population set might be the entire human proteome. The correct population set will depend on the biological question and is always a superset of the study sets.

The Ontologizer 2.0 performs statistical analysis for overrepresentation using the standard procedure based on summing the tail of the hypergeometric distribution. We refer to this procedure as the term-for-term approach. The Ontologizer implements a new procedure, the parent-child method, that has several advantages with respect to the term-for-term approach. Several methods for multiple-testing correction are provided. A more detailed account of these procedures is available at the Ontologizer homepage.
